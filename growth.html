Comparing Economic Growth

Countries worldwide have vastly different rates of economic growth, as well as different amounts of variance. Developed economies tend to growth steadily at a rate of 1-4%, without wild swings in either direction. Economies that depend heavily on single commodities like oil or cacao, meanwhile, can swing rapidly between booms and busts- growing 10% in one year then falling 8% in the next. Each country has a different story. This visualization allows you to compare countries across decades (in the scatterplot view) or continents within a decade (in the density plot view). Enjoy!
